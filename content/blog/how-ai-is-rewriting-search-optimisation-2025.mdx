Search is changing faster than at any point in the last two decades. For years, ranking on Google meant you were visible. That's no longer enough.

Today, millions of people are asking ChatGPT, Gemini and Perplexity for recommendations — and getting direct answers — without ever visiting a search results page. If your brand isn't showing up in those answers, you're invisible to a growing segment of your market.

## What Is AI Search and Why Does It Matter?

AI search refers to platforms that use large language models (LLMs) to generate direct, conversational answers to user queries. Instead of showing you a list of blue links, they synthesise information from across the web and present a single, confident answer.

The major players right now:

- **ChatGPT** (OpenAI) — now integrated with Bing search
- **Google Gemini** — powering Google AI Overviews in search results
- **Perplexity AI** — a dedicated AI search engine growing rapidly
- **Microsoft Copilot** — built into Windows and Bing

Each of these platforms pulls from different sources and uses different criteria to decide which brands and websites to recommend.

## How Is This Different From Traditional SEO?

Traditional SEO is about ranking in Google's 10 blue links. You optimise for keywords, build backlinks, improve page speed and create content — all to get Google's algorithm to rank you higher.

AI Optimisation (AIO) is about becoming a trusted source that AI models cite and recommend. The signals are different:

| Traditional SEO | AI Optimisation |
|---|---|
| Keyword rankings | Brand mentions |
| Backlink quantity | Source authority |
| On-page optimisation | Structured content |
| Click-through rate | Citation frequency |

## What Can You Do Right Now?

### 1. Build Topical Authority

AI models trust sources that comprehensively cover a topic. If you write one shallow blog post about SEO, you won't be cited. If you publish 20 deep, interconnected articles that cover every aspect of SEO, you become an authority.

### 2. Get Mentioned on Authoritative Sites

When a major publication mentions your brand in a positive context, AI models learn to associate your brand with trustworthiness. PR, digital PR and third-party reviews all matter.

### 3. Structure Your Content for AI Extraction

Write clearly. Use headings, lists and concise definitions. AI models extract information more reliably from well-structured content. Avoid walls of text.

### 4. Claim and Optimise Your Knowledge Panels

Your Google Knowledge Panel, LinkedIn company page and Wikipedia entry (if applicable) all feed into how AI models understand your brand.

### 5. Monitor Your AI Presence

Start tracking how often your brand appears in AI-generated answers. Tools like Profound and Brandwatch are starting to add AI mention tracking.

## The Bottom Line

Brands that invest in AIO now will have a significant head start. AI search is not a replacement for Google SEO — it's an additional layer that is growing faster than most businesses realise.

The question is not whether AI search will matter for your business. It already does. The question is whether you'll be visible when your customers ask the question.
